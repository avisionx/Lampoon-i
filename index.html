Presenting the Lampoon-i the Intelligent lamp

Lampoon-i detects you when you are in its range. Upon sensing someone, it switches itself on. One can also control it using the android app for the lamp which has various features as shown in video.

Independent Control

Lampoon-i can be independently controled using gestures simply by powering it up through direct AC supply.

Android Integration

Lampoon-i comes with its own custom app that can be used to take Lampoon-i one step further using Bluetooth technology.

Proximity Detection

Lampoon-i can be turned On from distance without App through proximity detection also, it is also a great security feature.

Set Lampoon-i's mood as you want.

Lampoon-i is able to perfeclty synchronize with your mood with just simple clicks on your smartphone. It works as follows:

  • Glows bright yellow when HAPPY.
  • Dazzels in bright green when SURPRISED.
  • Nullifies the blues when SLEEPY.
  • Switches to pink when ROMANTIC perfect for Valentines.

You can also select any color out of 16,777,215 colors from the color wheel through the app.

Lampoon-i has some amazing Transition features

  • Fading: Makes Lampoon-i randomly fade between millions of colors.
  • Pulsating: Makes Lampoon-i smoothly transition between 2 colours from OFF position.
  • Randomize: Makes Lapoon-i cycle between millions of colors with jump.

This feature allows Lampoon-i idle for festive lighting like for Diwali, Christmas etc.

Our Designing Process!

First we thought of making a rotating lamp which would spin using DC motors but as soon as we tested it in SCL lab we discovered flaw in our design. So we decided to make a much minimalistic and unique lamp.

Finally we came up with the design as shown by the paper prototype shown in right side. Now we were happy with the design and we started to implement it.

Some Thoughts!

Working on Lampoon-i was great fun. It helped me enhance my skills of working in group activities. Furthermore, it polished my crafting, designing as well as arduino programming skills.

In the end, we just felt that our design lacked some minor finishing touches like had we covered its sensor eyes as well as the body.
